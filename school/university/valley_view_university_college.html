Valley View

Valley View University College


Valley View University, a coeducational institution, started in 1979 as an Adventist Missionary College at Bekwai-Ashanti. The college was later relocated to it's present site, (Oyibi near Dodowa) and was renamed Valley View College (VVC).

In 1997 the National Accreditation Board gave recognition to VVC as the first private degree-awarding institution in the country, thus the Accreditation Association of Adventist Schools, Colleges and Universities based in Silver Spring, Maryland, USA.


Valley View University is therefore a member of a network of 98 Colleges and Universities operated by the Seventh-day Adventist Church worldwide. The mission of the University is to provide students, faculty and staff with an environment conducive to a balaced development of the intellectual, spiritual, physical and psycological dimensions of life in harmony with it's Christian heritage and African Culture.

In a context of liberal arts and professional curricula, the University emphasizes academic scholarship, vocational preparation and cultural understanding leading to a life of service to humanity.

Admissions are open to all applicants, provided they qualify for the programmes they are applying for irrespective of tribe, sex, nationality or religion. In 2004/05, student population was about 1,000.
