UCC

University of Cape Coast


The University of Cape Coast was established in 1962 and it is the third largest tertiary institution in Ghana. It is located at Cape Coast in the Central Region of Ghana. It was originally established to train graduate professional teachers for Ghana's second cycle institutions as well as the Ministry of Education.


Today, in addition to the above, the University offers a wide range of academic programmes in Business and Agriculture. It's vision is to become a Center of Excellence in Africa and the world for human resource and entrepreneurship development in education and related sectors.


Due to the influx of students in recent years, the University currently has limited accomodation in six halls of residence, a situation which implies that a large numer of students are non-residencial. Thus, the current policy is that, the first preference for accomodation is given to first year (Level 100) students.


The University's key strenght lies in it's Education programmes, however, the most sort after courses in this University are Bachelor of Commerce and Bachelor of Secretaryship and Management Studies. Recently, the Faculty of Science has been selected for a proposed Space Science Education Package in Africa for the promotion of Space Science on the promotion of Space Science Studies on the continent.


Another major development at the University of Cape Coast is that a School of Medical Sciences has started admiting students with effect from the 2007/08 academic year. For now, admission to the UCC Medical School programme is strictly on fee paying until further notice. In 2007/08, the fee per annum was GH 3,910.
