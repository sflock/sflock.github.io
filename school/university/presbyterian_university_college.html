PUC

Presbyterian University College


The Presbyterian University College started admission in October 2004. The first of the three campuses of the college is located at Abetifi, Kwahu, which boasts of cool climate conditions and modern amenities including Telephone and E-mail.

It has experienced lectures backed by a library with online facilities and although it has a Non-Resident College, the Housing Bureau assists students to locate suitable accommodation in its hostel at Nkwatia and Aduamoa. Like all Presbyterian educational institutions, PUC is open to the general Public and does not discriminate in terms of religion and gender. The College has also opened its Akwapem Campus at Akropong with effect from 2006/2007 academic year. The Academic year starts in October.


The Rural and Community Development Programme embraces a wide range of academic disciplines including Economics, Sociology, Planning and it is designed to provide a strong specialised skills in Rural and Community Development for students who upon graduation will obtain employment in organisations which deal with economic and social development issues such as NGOs and other agencies in rural development.


The Environment and Natural Resources Management Programme is a Multidisciplinary programme which includes best practices in the exploitation and utilisation of resources in the environment and how ensure a sound environmental protection.
