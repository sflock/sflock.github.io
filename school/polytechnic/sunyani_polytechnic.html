Sunyani Polytechnic


Sunyani Polytechnic, the premier tertiary institution in the Brong Ahafo region of Ghana, is the successor to Sunyani Technical Institute which was established by the Government of Ghana in 1967.

In 1997 the institute was upgraded to its present Polytechnic status by the Polytechnic Law of 1992 (PNDC Law 321) and tasked to provide tertiary education through full-time courses in the field of manufacturing, commerce, science, technology, applied science, applied arts and such other areas as may be determined by the authority responsible for higher education. The PNDC Law 321 was repealed by an act of Parliament, Polytechnics Act, 2007 (Act. 745). The Act expanded the operational realm of the Polytechnic to include the introduction of degree and higher degree programmes and provide opportunities for skills development, applied research and publication of research findings.

The school's vision is to become a centre of excellence for the provision career-focused practically oriented middle level manpower training for the socio-economic development of the region and the nation in general.

Mision Statement

Sunyani Polytechnic is a public institution of higher learning that is committed to the provision of career-focused education and training at the tertiary level with hands on experience and entrepreneurship development to fill the middle level manpower needs of the country.
